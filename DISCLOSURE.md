# Document Tier and Redaction Policy

This document describes exactly how the materials in this repository have been handled. The purpose is to make the modifications transparent so that no claim of altered evidence can be sustained.

## Three tiers

Every document exists in three forms.

Tier 1 — Original. The document as received from the issuing party (medical provider, VA, insurer, federal channel). Originals are retained privately by the relator. They are produced on legitimate request through verified channels.

Tier 2 — OCR copy. The original processed through optical character recognition software to make text searchable. No content has been added, removed, or altered. OCR may introduce minor character recognition errors; these are artifacts of the OCR process and not modifications to the original. OCR copies are retained privately.

Tier 3 — Posted version. The OCR copy with specific redactions applied per the policy below. Posted versions are what appear in this repository.

## What is redacted in posted versions

- Social Security Numbers
- Account numbers, member numbers, claim numbers (where these would enable identification or contact attempts against third parties)
- Dates of birth and full birthdates of any party other than the relator
- Names of patients other than the relator
- Names of administrative or junior staff who appear in records incidentally and are not subjects of the case
- Personal contact information (home addresses, personal phone numbers) of any party

## What is NOT redacted

- Names of medical providers who are subjects of the case (defendants named in federal submissions)
- Names of medical providers whose participation is documented in the records
- Dates of service, billing dates, and submission dates
- Bill amounts, procedure codes, and diagnosis codes
- Provider business names and practice addresses
- The relator's name and identifying information, by the relator's choice

## Where names were used inconsistently in the original records

In some records, billing was submitted under a name different from the practitioner who performed the service. Where this is documented, both names are retained in the posted version with annotation noting the discrepancy. This is itself part of what the case documents.

## How to verify the originals

Any party with a legitimate need to verify the originals against the posted versions can request verification through:

- Senator Ashley Moody's office (Inquiry #2017816)
- US DOJ National Fraud Enforcement Division
- The relator directly, with appropriate credentials and verification

The relator does not provide originals to unverified parties.
